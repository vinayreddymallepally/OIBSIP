# Task 5: Research Report on Social Engineering Attacks

## 1. Introduction
Social engineering is the art of manipulating people so they give up confidential information. Unlike technical hacking, it targets the "human operating system."

## 2. Common Social Engineering Techniques
### A. Phishing
* **Definition:** Sending fraudulent emails that appear to be from a reputable source to steal data.
* **Impact:** Credential theft and malware delivery.
* **Mitigation:** Use Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) and verify email headers.

### B. Pretexting
* **Definition:** An attacker creates a fabricated scenario (the pretext) to steal a victim's personal information (e.g., a "bank employee" calling to verify your SSN).
* **Mitigation:** Never share sensitive info over an unsolicited call; call the official number back.

### C. Baiting
* **Definition:** Leaving a malware-infected physical device (like a USB drive) in a public place, hoping a victim will plug it into their computer.
* **Mitigation:** Disable "Auto-run" on devices and never plug in unknown USBs.

## 3. Case Study: The 2020 Twitter Hack
Attackers used "Vishing" (Voice Phishing) to trick Twitter employees into giving up access to internal administrative tools, leading to the takeover of high-profile accounts.

## 4. Recommendations for Prevention
1. **Security Awareness Training:** Regular drills to help employees spot fake emails.
2. **Strict Verification Protocols:** Always verify the identity of anyone asking for data.
